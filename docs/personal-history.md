### Convergence — What All Three Agree On

1. **The sadness is real, not an excuse.** The "excuse vs. real" framing is a trap. Both coexist. The sadness is genuine AND he still has to function — but the functioning shouldn't come at the cost of never feeling it.
2. **You're not missing her — you're missing feeling chosen.** The ex represents the last time he felt the spark, the softness, the being-seen. Knowing this intellectually doesn't resolve it. It resolves when someone else gets to witness the grief.
3. **The hyperperformance is both building AND numbing.** Not either/or. The achievement is real. The avoidance driving it is also real. They're not mutually exclusive, and refusing to see both keeps the crash cycle going.
4. **Isolation is the structural root.** Before grief work, before therapy frameworks, before mindset shifts — he needs to be around people regularly. Not engineered connection. Just consistent presence in the same place, same time, letting it become something organic.
5. **Grief needs a witness.** 5.5 years of private grief calcifies. It needs to be spoken aloud to someone who can hold it. The dreams slow down after that. Not before.
6. **The sensitivity that causes the sadness IS the raw material for everything he wants.** Spark, charisma, love — they come from the same nerve endings as the grief. You can't numb one without numbing the other.
7. **There's a flat stretch between numbing and aliveness.** It's boring. It feels like being broken. It lasts months. He needs to know it's coming so he doesn't interpret "flat" as "failed."
8. **The fuel source has to change.** He's running on cortisol and self-punishment. The bar needs to come down temporarily — not because he's weak, but because sustainable drive requires a different engine: desire, connection, a life he actually wants to live in.

### Still Unresolved

1. **Active numbing vs. building — where's the line?** Tony never fully concedes that the grind is avoidance. The therapist never fully concedes that it's also genuinely constructive. The functional difference matters and they didn't settle it. How much should he actually work? Nobody gave a number. Nobody could.

Future Self says the framing of "go to therapy" triggers the optimizer in him and gets deprioritized. They agreed on the need for a witness but not on whether that witness needs to be a professional.
4. **The flat stretch — how to survive it concretely.** All three named it. None of them offered a real playbook beyond "lower the bar" and "show up somewhere consistently." What does a Tuesday look like in the flat stretch? What does he do at 2pm when he's not sad and not alive and not working? They left that empty.
5. **When does the grief actually shift?** Future Self says year six. But he also says it could compress with a witness. How much? Months? A year? The timeline is genuinely uncertain and none of them could promise anything, which is honest but unsatisfying.
6. **Can he call someone right now?** Future Self conceded he hung up after two rings during this same period. If reaching out requires feeling safe enough to be seen, and he doesn't feel safe, the "go be around people" advice has a bootstrapping problem that none of them fully solved.
