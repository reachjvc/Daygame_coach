### Approach Anxiety is Legacy Software

**ID:** em-01
**Tier:** 1 (Daygame-specific)
**Priority:** Phase 1 (Foundation)
**Hook:** "Your fear of approaching isn't broken. It's calibrated perfectly — for 50,000 years ago."

**Contract:**
- **Thesis:** Approach anxiety is an evolutionary adaptation to tribal social dynamics that misfires in modern anonymous environments.
- **Target reader:** Men who experience approach anxiety and want to understand why it feels so disproportionate to the actual risk.
- **Must include:**
  - Evolutionary psychology of social fear (Part XXII)
  - Tribe size and reputation stakes (Dunbar's number)
  - The mismatch hypothesis: ancestral vs. modern environments
  - Reframe: "miscalibrated" not "broken"
- **Must NOT include:**
  - Specific approach techniques or openers
  - "Just approach anyway" advice without understanding
  - Dismissing the fear as irrational
- **Tone:** Scientific third-person with evolutionary framing

**Research context:**
- Primary: docs/research/emotional-management.md
  - Part VIII: Approach Anxiety & Social Fear
  - Part XXII: Evolutionary Psychology (Sections 22.1-22.3)
- Key citations:
  - Dunbar, R.I.M. (1992) on group size constraints
  - Buss, D.M. (1994/2016) on mating strategies
  - Sapolsky, R.M. (2005) on social hierarchy

**Outline:**
1. **Hook/Intro:** Present the paradox — intelligent men feel terror approaching strangers
2. **The ancestral environment:** Why this fear made sense for 99% of human history
3. **The mismatch:** Modern anonymity vs. tribal accountability
4. **The reframe:** From "I'm broken" to "I'm miscalibrated"
5. **What this means practically:** Fear as information, not verdict
6. **Conclusion:** Liberation through understanding
